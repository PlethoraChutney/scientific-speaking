Use your final slide to summarize, like this:

  • Consider your audience before designing your talk
  • Plan your slides before you make them
  • If you like how it looks and people can read it, do it
  • Short, informative title
  • As much real estate for figures as possible
  • Talk figures, not paper figures
  • Use animation to hold the audience's hand
  • Use checkpoint slides, especially if your narrative is nonlinear

For the rest of class, in groups of two or three:

  1. Pick a topic with a clear main message or idea
  2. Develop a narrative to effectively communicate that idea
  3. Create one title slide, one content slide, and one checkpoint slide which might be used during your narrative
    • You can use whatever software you want
    • You do not need to make a full talk, just one of each type of slide
    • Feel free to describe a figure with text instead of making your own
    • Be sure to credit any images you use
  4. Submit your group's slides to Sakai (one submission per group)
